# Credex: Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

## 1. The Core Problem
Startups are silently hemorrhaging cash on redundant AI tooling (Copilot + Cursor + ChatGPT + Claude + Gemini). Because these tools are often expensed individually by engineers on corporate cards, finance teams lack visibility into the overlapping features and wasted seats.

## 2. Target Audience
- **Primary**: Startup Founders & Fractional CFOs at Series A/B companies (50-200 employees).
- **Secondary**: VP of Engineering / CTO looking to standardize their team's tooling.

## 3. Acquisition Channels

### A. Product-Led SEO (Engineering/Finance crossover)
- Create programmatic SEO pages comparing tools: "Cursor vs. GitHub Copilot 2026", "Claude Pro vs ChatGPT Enterprise ROI".
- Offer the free **AI Spend Audit** tool as the primary Call-to-Action on these pages.

### B. The "Viral" Audit Loop
1. User takes the free audit on Credex.
2. User sees they are wasting $15,000/yr.
3. User shares the auto-generated OpenGraph link in their founder Slack group.
4. The OG image dynamically displays the exact dollar amount saved, prompting other founders to click and run their own audit.

### C. Direct Outreach (Outbound)
- Scrape LinkedIn for newly hired "Head of Finance" or "VP Engineering" at funded startups.
- **Message**: "Noticed you just took over the stack at [Company]. On average, we find $12k in redundant AI spend for teams your size. Run a free audit in 2 mins."

## 4. The Wedge & Expansion
- **The Wedge**: The free audit identifies the waste.
- **The Monetization**: Credex offers to automatically execute the downgrades and pool the licenses into our wholesale reseller tier for a percentage of the savings (or a flat SaaS fee).
