# Credex Unit Economics & Monetization

## 1. Pricing Model
Credex operates on a **Shared Savings / Gainshare Model** combined with a freemium SaaS tier.

### Free Tier: The Audit
- The initial audit and generation of the "Optimization Playbook" is 100% free.
- **Goal**: High volume lead generation and trust building.

### Paid Tier 1: The Execution (One-Time)
- Credex provides a "Done-For-You" service where we log into the user's Okta/Google Workspace, identify inactive seats, and cancel redundant licenses.
- **Cost**: 20% of the first year's identified savings.
- *Example*: We find $10,000 in savings. We charge a one-time fee of $2,000.

### Paid Tier 2: The Syndicate (Recurring SaaS)
- Credex acts as a license reseller. We pool our clients together to negotiate enterprise wholesale rates with OpenAI, Anthropic, and GitHub.
- **Cost**: $99/month platform fee + users pay Credex for the licenses at a 15% discount off retail.
- Credex pockets the margin between the wholesale rate and the 15% discount rate.

## 2. Estimated Unit Economics

- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: ~$150 (Driven heavily down by the viral OG image loop and free audit tool).
- **Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)**:
  - Average startup wastes ~$6,000/yr in AI tooling.
  - 20% gainshare = $1,200 immediate revenue.
- **LTV / CAC Ratio**: With an LTV of $1,200 and a CAC of $150, the ratio is **8:1**, indicating highly efficient capital deployment.

## 3. Key Risks
- **Vendor Consolidation**: If Microsoft or Google successfully bundles all AI tools natively into their suites, the standalone AI tool market (and thus the waste) may shrink.
- **API Changes**: Dependence on vendor APIs to programmatically cancel/provision seats.
